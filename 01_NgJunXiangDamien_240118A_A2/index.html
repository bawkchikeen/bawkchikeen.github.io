Foraging

Foraging is one of the most basic ways to get edible food while in the wild. Foraging involves identifying and harvesting wild edibles while avoiding the poisonous ones. Wild edibles could be anything from berries to mushrooms to even flowers!

Common Wild Edibles

Cattails are common and easy to harvest, but they also have a poisonous look-alike, the Iris plant.
The Iris is flat at the base and makes a fan shape where all the leave meet the ground, while edible Cattails have a central vein called a midrib around which its leaves form.
