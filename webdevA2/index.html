Foraging

Foraging is one of the most basic ways to get edible food while in the wild. Foraging involves identifying and harvesting wild edibles while avoiding the poisonous ones. Wild edibles could be anything from berries to mushrooms to even flowers!

Common Wild Edibles

Cattails are common and easy to harvest, but they also have a poisonous look-alike, the Iris plant.
The Iris is flat at the base and makes a fan shape where all the leave meet the ground, while edible Cattails have a central vein called a midrib around which its leaves form.
Here's a video showing more common and useful Wild Edibles for survival!

Another common plant that is edible is the Dandelion! Even though they're fairly common, I was personally surprised that this humble flower is actually something many people use to make salads with!

Fishing

Fishing comes in many forms, not just the typical fishing rod and hook! Although we'll be doing that later, I was thinking we should look at one other forms of 'fishing'!

Trapping!

With just the concept of baiting and funnelling, you can create really simple traps to catch fish! A really simple one to keep in mind is to use plastic bottles, big or small both work. Simply cut then fold in the top of a bottle to create a funnel, then put bait in it. Bait doesn't have to be anyhing fancy! Whatever you have leftover is usually good enough :D

Making a Makeshift Rod

As shown in the video, there are many useful materials out there in the wild for us to take advantage of, we simply need the know how! Dry grass is also a viable material for creating rope, as long as you can get long strands of some kind of fibre, you could probably create a rudimentary but useful fishing rod!
